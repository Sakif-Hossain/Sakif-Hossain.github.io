About Me

Driven by my childhood love for games, I am a creative and dedicated individual pursuing a career as a software and game developer. Currently in my third year of studying Computing Science with a Specialization in Software Practice at the University of Alberta, I am also expanding my expertise through a game development certificate.

Now, I am immersed in the world of web development, where I develop my skills in front-end and back-end technologies such as Svelte, React, JavaScript, Django, and PostgreSQL. I am more passionate about back-end development and I hope to work as a full-stack developer in the future. I am also proficient in programming languages such as C, C++, C#, Python, Java, DBMS like SQLite and MongoDB, and tools like Linux, Git, and GitHub.

Furthermore, I possess hands-on experience with Unity, RPG Maker MV, and Godot game engines. I am eager to join a game studio in a programmer position and contribute my skills to creating immersive and captivating gaming experiences. You can check out my game projects or my software projects and learn about what I do.

In my free time I love to cook, travel or play games. My favourite cuisine is Bangladeshi cuisine. So far I love Bali as a vacation spot due to its amazing natural sites. I love to explore new games but my favourite genre is FPS and RPG like Assassins Creed, Call of Duty and God of War.

Game Projects

Daybreak

  • Duration: 4 months.
  • Role: Programmer and artist.
  • Description: Collaborated as a multi-disciplinary group of five individuals to create an independent 2D action-adventure RPG game called Daybreak in CMPUT 250.
    Daybreak offers players an engaging experience as they take on the role of a lone ranger stranded on an uncharted planet. The primary objective is to repair a malfunctioning spacecraft by strategically sourcing essential resources within the alien environment. The game unfolds as a methodical journey, requiring players to navigate the challenges of the unknown planet, make informed decisions, and execute precise actions to secure a successful return to space.
  • Responsibilities: As a programmer and artist, my responsibilities included designing and implementing various game mechanisms. Notably, I developed a lock system that required finding specific keys to unlock. I also focused on player movements and level design, ensuring a seamless gaming experience. Additionally, I performed quality assurance testing, debugging, and took charge of deploying the game and creating the website.
  • Technologies used: RPG Maker MV, Git, GitHub, JavaScript.

Solar System Model

  • Duration: 1 week.
  • Description: This is my first project from an online course learning Unity. This project involved the creation of a solar system model, showcasing an intricately designed mock representation of planets and stars suspended in space.
  • Responsibilities: I was responsible for creating Jupiter, Saturn, Mars, Venus, Mercury, Uranus, and Neptune. I tried to make the distance and the size of each planet relative to the solar system. I added sounds to Saturn and Mars and changed the position of the cameras to make it look better.
